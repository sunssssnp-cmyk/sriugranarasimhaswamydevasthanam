In the history of Sri Ugra Narasimha Swamy Temple, the Dhwaja Sthambha (flagstaff) holds a sacred and significant position. It is believed that the original flagstaff was installed nearly 200 to 300 years ago.

Construction: The original Dhwaja Sthambha was made from a Neem tree trunk, as was customary in ancient times.

Lack of Written Records: There are no written records indicating the exact date of installation or the individuals who conducted the original consecration.

Copper Plate Evidence: During the removal of the old flagstaff, a copper plate was discovered beneath it. Although the inscriptions had faded over time, village elders confirmed that it bore the inscription ‚ÄúSri Ugra Narasimha Swamy Temple‚Äù.

This copper plate is regarded as an important historical proof of the temple‚Äôs antiquity.

üôè Reinstallation in 2002

After serving the temple for centuries, the decision to replace the old Neem-wood flagstaff with a new one was realized in the year 2002.

Year of Installation: 2002

This Dhwaja Sthambha reinstallation marked the first major step toward the renovation, revival, and overall development of the Sri Ugra Narasimha Swamy Temple.
