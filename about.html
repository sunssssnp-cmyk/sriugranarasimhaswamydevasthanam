Eight Arms of Ugra Narasimha Swaroopa: The idol of Lord Narasimha Swamy in the Sanginenipalli Guttala cave is seen in the most fierce form with eight arms. The scene of the Swami's killing of the demon Hiranyakasyapu on his thigh is clearly visible in this statue The first two hands see Hiranyakashipu lying on his thigh and tearing his stomach apart. Then there are two hands: Hiranyakashipu's head and legs held tightly without moving. Then there are two hands: Pulling up the intestines coming out of Hiranyakashipu's stomach and holding them in the neck like a garland of flowers. The last two hands: The Lord is seen holding his weapons, the conch shell and the Sudarshan Chakra, in fierce anger (with fury). Archaeological authorities estimate that the statue dates back to the 10th - 12th century (Chalukya style). Image of Goddess Lakshmi: Goddess Chenchu Lakshmi is standing next to the Lord and Goddess Lakshmi is seen in the cave next to her.
